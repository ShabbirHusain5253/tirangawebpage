History

Icons of Indian Independence

Civil Disobedience Movement

The Civil Disobedience Movement was launched in 1930, and it commenced with the famous Dandi March when Gandhi left the Sabarmati Ashram at Ahmedabad on foot. Upon reaching Dandi, Gandhi broke the salt law. This led to the Salt Satyagraha Movement across the country.

A Tryst with Destiny

'A Tryst with Destiny'; the first speech delivered by the Prime Minister of India at the stroke of midnight on 14th August 1947.

History of the Tricolor

The year 1931 was a landmark in the history of the flag. A resolution was passed adopting a tricolor flag as our national flag. This flag, the forbear of the present one, was saffron, white and green with Mahatma Gandhi's spinning wheel at the center.

Tricolor

Tricolour importance: Indian flag colours meaning and chakra significance.

The saffron colour signifies the strength and courage of the country.

The white colour indicates peace and truth.
The chakra shows that there is life in movement and death in stagnation.

The green colour shows the fertility, growth and auspiciousness of the land.

Creation Of Tiranga

The first iteration for a national flag for India was created by Bhagini Nivedita also known as Sister Nivedita. It had the colors Red; signifying the freedom struggle and Yellow; denoting victory. It was hoisted up in Parsee Bagan in Kolkata on August 7, 1906.
In 1921, a young man named Pingali Vankayya created another flag with two colors; red and green to represent the two major religions of India, the color white was added on the recommendation of Mahatma Gandhi to represent all the other communities of India as well as the Charkha to represent our struggle.
The final version of the tricolor was created in 1931 also by Vankayya devoid of any religous connotations this time. This flag had the colors Saffron; to signify strength, the White represented Truth while Green at the bottom represented fertility. The charkha was replaced by the Ashok Chakra. This flag was finally adopted in 1947 and was first hoisted in the same year.
